History of IG Drones

2018

The DJI Phantom 4 RTK was launched, setting a new standard for drone mapping and surveying with its high-precision GNSS module. This drone became a favorite in industries like construction, agriculture, and land surveying due to its ability to capture highly accurate data, reducing the need for ground control points.

2019

The DJI Mavic Mini was introduced, targeting the consumer market with its ultra-compact design, weighing under 250 grams, which exempted it from certain regulations in many countries. Despite its small size, it offered impressive features like a 12MP camera capable of shooting 2.7K video, making it ideal for casual users and hobbyists.

2020

The Parrot Anafi USA was launched, tailored for first responders and professionals needing a robust, portable drone with advanced imaging capabilities. It featured a 32x zoom camera and thermal imaging, making it suitable for search and rescue missions, surveillance, and inspections in various environments

2021

The DJI Air 2S was released, boasting a 1-inch sensor capable of 20MP stills and 5.4K video. This drone combined high-quality imaging with advanced flight modes like MasterShots, enabling users to capture cinematic content effortlessly. It bridged the gap between consumer and professional drones with its versatile features.

2022

The Autel Evo Lite+ entered the market, offering a variable aperture camera (f/2.8-f/11) and 50MP resolution. It was designed for enthusiasts and semi-professionals seeking high-quality photography and videography in a portable drone, with a strong emphasis on low-light performance.

2023

The Skydio 2+ was introduced, showcasing next-level autonomous flight capabilities with its advanced AI-driven obstacle avoidance and tracking. This drone was particularly popular for action sports and cinematic filming, where precise and dynamic camera movements were essential.

2024

The DJI Inspire 3 was launched, catering to the professional filmmaking industry with its full-frame 8K camera, interchangeable lenses, and dual-control system. It set a new benchmark for aerial cinematography, allowing for highly detailed and cinematic shots previously achievable only with much larger and more expensive equipment..
